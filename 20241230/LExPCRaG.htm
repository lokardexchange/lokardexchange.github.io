Class Rules and Regulations

Maintain professionalism, safety, and integrity at all times. Policies below supplement institutional guidelines.

Stand at attention when I enter the room. You will also be instructed to stand at attention when a higher-ranking individual arrives. Upon the command “Attention!”, immediately stop what you are doing, face forward, and listen.

  • Cheating is strictly prohibited. If caught, your paper will be confiscated and you will be referred to the disciplinary officer.
  • Side-glancing during assessments is considered cheating.

A class mayor will be elected to maintain order, relay concerns, and ensure the classroom is clean before dismissal.

The class mayor and deputy will also act as moderators of the class Facebook page.

  • No extension of deadlines unless extremely necessary and formally communicated.
  • Submit on or before the deadline; plan ahead for contingencies.
  • Bring a black Pentel pen and a correction tape during exams.
  • No borrowing of correction tape is allowed.
  • All excused absences must be supported with valid documentation and presented on the next class you attend.
  • Acceptable documents include a medical certificate (illness) or a copy of a death certificate (immediate family).

Read and follow instructions thoroughly. Stay alert to verbal and non-verbal cues from the instructor.

  • Maintain a clean workspace and properly dispose of waste.
  • Observe safety and emergency procedures; follow health protocols when applicable.
  • Practice respectful communication and teamwork.

No Gifting: Students must not give material items to the teacher. Professional boundaries are to be maintained.

  • You may visit during consultation hours to inquire about your grades.
  • Requests for rectification will only be entertained before the final exam; bring supporting proof (returned outputs, quizzes, lab work).
  • Incomplete (INC): Missing major requirements result in an INC grade. Even if you passed the course, failure to submit major requirements will keep your grade as INC until resolved per policy.
  • Collaboration is a shared responsibility. Participate actively and equitably.
  • Group performance and individual contribution will be evaluated.

No Spoon-feeding: Supplement lectures with your own reading and research. Take initiative in exploring references and deepening understanding.

Focus on learning, not merely on grades. Engage with concepts, ask questions, and strive for mastery.

  • No horseplay. Handle instruments with care; speak quietly.
  • Always bring required protective equipment and follow instructions diligently.
  • Safety is a priority—report hazards immediately.
  • Expect a short assessment every meeting.
  • Half of the items count as a quiz; the other half as recitation.
  • Bring your recitation card, a 0.25 sheet of yellow paper, and a black pen every session.
  • It is the student’s responsibility to obtain missed lessons and announcements from classmates.
  • For missed assessments, completion expires after the next session you attend. Complete requirements promptly.
  • Turn on your camera and be ready to unmute when called.
  • Join our Discord server during scheduled class time.
  • If the instructor does not arrive within 10 minutes, you may leave.
  • Online sessions typically include: (1) discussion/instructions, (2) activity, and (3) reading.

Maintain silence during class. Refrain from talking to classmates unless instructed. Communicate primarily with the instructor. Move quietly. Only involuntary sounds (e.g., sneezing or coughing) are excused.

  • Follow the required format for all submissions.
  • Include your name and date unless otherwise instructed.
  • Put devices on silent mode.
  • Smartphones may be used only for emergencies or verified course-related searches.
  • You may verify class information online when appropriate.
