Safety Procedures and Instructions

Immediate Actions:

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On
  • Drop to the ground to avoid being knocked over.
  • Take cover under sturdy furniture or against an interior wall away from windows.
  • Hold on until shaking stops.
  • Stay Indoors (if already inside): Do not run outside. Stay away from glass, hanging objects, or anything that may fall.
  • If Outdoors: Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, poles, or wires. Stay low and protect your head.
  • If in a Vehicle: Pull over to a safe location and remain inside.

Secondary Actions:

  • Conduct a headcount and check for injuries.
  • Be alert for aftershocks.
  • Do not use elevators.
  • Shut off utilities if trained and necessary.

Post-Incident Actions:

  • Evacuate if unsafe and go to the designated evacuation area.
  • Report injuries and damages to emergency responders.
  • Await official all-clear before re-entering.
  • Follow emergency management instructions.

Immediate Actions:

  • Raise the Alarm: Activate fire alarm and inform authorities.
  • Evacuate Immediately: Use the nearest safe exit. Do not use elevators. Crawl if needed to avoid smoke.
  • Close doors behind you to contain the fire.
  • Check doors for heat: Use the back of your hand. If hot, do not open.
  • Assist others: Help persons with disabilities or those needing aid.

Secondary Actions:

  • Conduct accountability check at evacuation site.
  • Do not re-enter the building unless cleared.

Post-Incident Actions:

  • Report trapped individuals or hazards to officials.
  • Give first aid if qualified.
  • Document the incident for reporting.

Secondary Actions:

  • Assist in calm evacuation.
  • Give first aid if safe and trained.

Post-Incident Actions:

  • Fully cooperate with authorities.
  • Account for all personnel.
  • Preserve scene if asked for investigation.
  • Join debrief and review.
  • Stay calm and think clearly: Panic spreads, discipline saves lives.
  • Know your exits: Always be aware of escape routes.
  • Train regularly: Drills are essential for survival.
  • Report hazards or suspicious activity immediately.
